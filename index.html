A Sherbrooke-based communication strategist with a track record in content production, digital campaigns, and stakeholder-driven communications across the private and institutional sectors.

Originally from the Democratic Republic of Congo, I grew up navigating multiple cultures and languages which shaped my ability to bridge unrelated worlds. My work spans communications strategy, content production, and business development for clients across tech, gaming, social economy, and international markets..

My approach combines editorial discipline with strategic framing: I think about audiences before I think about formats. Whether developing a social media strategy, producing content around research milestones, or coordinating visibility across institutional partners, I focus on what moves people to act, follow, or believe.

I've spent the last several years building one consistent skill set through different contexts: knowing how to say the right thing to the right audience, in the right format, at the right moment. That's what communication strategy actually is.

Most people rush to action because it feels productive. But the real leverage is in those quiet hours before anything begins when mapping the terrain, questioning assumptions, searching for the angle nobody else considered. That's where great work is born. What I've learned is that most communication problems are actually positioning problems. Organizations don't struggle to produce content. They struggle to decide what they stand for, who they're talking to, and what they want those people to do next. I help them answer those three questions before anything gets written.

I spend serious time hunting for new ideas. Reading widely. Connecting dots across disciplines. Cultivating what a restless curiosity. I want to understand how things work, why they work, what happens when you combine them in unexpected ways.

But curiosity without drive is just entertainment. That competitive fire that demands of excellence for myself, a refuses to settle, that sees obstacles as fuel. I want to win. Not for ego, but because winning means the work mattered, the effort counted, the impact was real.

Here's what I believe: competition isn't the enemy of meaning. Channeled properly, that drive becomes the engine for work that actually helps people. Work that moves things forward. Work you can be proud of when you look back.

I want to build, to solve, to create—with people who feel the same fire and care
